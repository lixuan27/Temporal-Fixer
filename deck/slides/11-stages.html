4 · Full training process · click a stage for its gate

Five stages, each with a numeric gate. No stage starts before the previous one passes.

S0 · Calibrate the frozen editor 0 GPU-hours of training

Do: reconstruction probe at zero edit; sweep seed policy, step count, guidance; measure draft flicker amplitude and character; verify structure-exactness by eye.

Gate ✓ passed

Draft flicker ≤ 10 at shared seed (got 9.40, from 48.4) · structure-exact confirmed · guidance shown to be a no-op · 4-step distilled editor at 2.2 s/frame.

Checkpoint artifact

A frozen, documented editor protocol: 4 steps, shared seed, per-clip best-of-N seed selection (+8% edit cost, measured to improve on-promptness).

If it had failed

Fall back to flow-coupled noise (noise follows content) — strictly better than a global shared seed for a moving camera.

S1 · Quantify the train–test gap the mentor's central question

Do: generate identity / mild / drastic drafts on the same clips; measure flicker magnitude and the character spectrum for each; decide the training draft distribution.

Gate ✓ passed

Same kind of flicker confirmed (33% vs 34% high-frequency share); amplitude gap quantified (2.07 vs 7.37); the 16-cell sweep proved prompts cannot close it without breaking the target.

Checkpoint artifact

A calibrated, appearance-preserving jitter model whose moments match the real editor's flicker statistics.

Residual risk

If jitter under-mimics the editor, fall back to mixing a small set of real mild-edit drafts (with a warped-median pseudo-target) into training.

S2 · Train the fixer — source-free, draft-anchored running now

Do: three arms on real reconstruction pairs, all source-free (source slot := draft): (A) residual 0.2 / σ_a 0.10 · (B) residual 0.5 — stronger appearance anchor · (C) σ_a 0.05 — less regeneration. 1.3B causal DiT, bf16, 33 frames, batch 4, checkpoints every 500 steps, saturation expected ≈1000 steps.

Gate — must pass BOTH

Deflicker: ≥30% reduction on real edit drafts. Anti-reversion: edit preservation ≥ 0.93 (CLIP-to-draft). Both, on watched video — a night→day driving edit must still be daytime.

Kill criteria

Any arm whose output is blurred, whose dynamics collapse, or whose edit is reverted is cancelled at the 500-step checkpoint — no waiting for the full run.

Contingency

If all three arms revert: switch the target from the clean source to a styled target (target ≠ source by construction), which makes reversion structurally impossible.

S3 · Driving domain + long-horizon streaming

Do: rebuild the reconstruction corpus on driving footage; retrain the winning arm; attach the rolling KV cache with position re-indexing; measure the per-chunk latency breakdown and the drift curve over minutes.

Gate

Flicker and edit preservation hold on driving clips · drift slope over a minute-long stream < 1/3 of an end-to-end autoregressive editor · constant per-chunk latency confirmed.

Why we expect it to hold

Driving scenes are rigid-structure dominated; flicker scales with high-frequency organic texture (foliage/water: 8–17; rigid structure: <1). The domain is favourable.

Contingency

If the fixer alone cannot hold minute-scale streams, pin a small frame sink in the cache and re-index; the editor cannot drift, so any drift is base-rollout drift and is addressable there.

S4 · Benchmarks and the head-to-head

Do: OpenVE-Bench (48) + FiVE-Bench (40) + VBench temporal + V2V-Bench + driving. The competitor's checkpoint is executed by us on our videos with our protocol. Smoothness is never reported without motion.

Gate

Temporal metrics at or near the dedicated edit-supervised editor while preserving more motion, at zero paired data and single-step cost; plus cross-editor generalization with no retraining.

Known standing

Already: smoothness 0.979 vs 0.993, subject consistency 0.918 vs 0.983, motion 0.375 vs 0.25. Their remaining edge is per-frame imaging quality.

Honest boundary

Per-frame quality is upper-bounded by the editor's re-render, not by the fixer. If it must improve, the lever is the front-end editor — we will say so rather than tune the fixer.

Compute discipline: every job goes through the scheduler; each new run is smoke-tested for utilization before a long submission; failed runs may not be resubmitted without a written root cause and a changed config. 11
