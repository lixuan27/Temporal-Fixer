1 · Intro — Challenge

Why the standard routes stall.

Four failure modes, each of which we have now either measured on our own runs or reproduced from the open-source competitor.

End-to-end streaming editors the direct competitor

  • Need paired edit data. It must be synthesized by a bigger model and filtered by a VLM — and bad pairs actively hurt the fine-tune.
  • Edit-path drift. Editing lives inside the autoregressive state, so it drifts over minutes. Positional re-indexing and attention-sink tricks fix the base rollout, not the edit path.
  • Smoothness bought with motion. Reproduced on our videos: motion smoothness 0.993 but dynamic degree 0.25 — a near-static video is trivially "consistent".

Inference-time consistency & naive filtering

  • Feature/flow propagation methods are non-causal (need keyframes or the whole clip) → cannot stream; the consistency prior is hand-designed.
  • Optical-flow smoothing, measured: −43% flicker, but it blurs and it erases the edit (edit preservation 0.80 → 0.75). It cannot tell an intended edit from an artifact.

Wall 1 · the single-sided ceiling we hit it

Optimizing the fixer alone just slides along one flicker↔quality frontier. Five independent avenues, all falsified:

latent high-pass loss · pixel perceptual loss · 2× training duration · flow-guided temporal loss · 10× capacity (1.3B → 14B). The apparent 14B gain was exactly reproduced by a zero-training pixel blend of the small model — an interpolation artifact.

Wall 2 · the self-supervision trap we hit it

If the clean source is both the target and a conditioning input, the model learns the shortcut "copy the source" — and at test time it undoes the edit.

Measured: flicker 6.13 → 2.04 (looks excellent) while edit preservation collapsed 0.959 → 0.742; a night→day driving edit was reverted to night. Metrics cheered, the eye killed it.
