1 · Intro — Insight (a) · I

Do not ask a causal video model to edit. Ask it to fix.

Insight 1 · Re-partition the entropy

Editing semantics belong to a model with no temporal state; temporal coherence belongs to a model with only temporal state. Splitting them turns a high-entropy generation problem into a low-entropy residual problem.

Inspiration. The "bad reconstruction in, clean video out" refiner pattern (a noisy 3D-Gaussian reconstruction refined by a video model) + the measured fact that a per-frame editor is structure-exact but flickery.

Insight 2 · Drift immunity is architectural, not a patch

A per-frame editor has no recurrent state: it has frame-wise stochastic inconsistency but no accumulated drift. Every frame's draft is a fresh anchor that pulls the causal fixer back on track.

Inspiration. The open problem stated in the weekly meeting: positional (RoPE re-indexing) and content (attention-sink) fixes cannot repair drift that originates in the editing/conditioning path. We remove that path from the loop entirely.

Insight 3 · Most "editing flicker" is not editing — it is the sampling protocol

A reconstruction probe (zero edit, prompt = "change nothing") flickers at 26 — while the natural-video floor is 1.0. Share one noise seed across frames and it drops to 7.0. Apply a drastic style edit at that shared seed: still 7.1.

The edit contributes almost nothing. The independent noise draw was producing the flicker.

The free win

Zero training. Editor-side protocol only. It beat the entire previous (editor + fixer) system.
